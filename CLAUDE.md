What This Tool Does
This tool helps privacy and legal teams at mid-market companies (100–500 employees) understand their obligations under the EU AI Act. Users submit a description of an AI use case their organization is deploying or considering. The tool classifies that use case by EU AI Act risk tier (Unacceptable, High, Limited, Minimal), maps it to the applicable regulatory requirements for that tier, identifies compliance gaps based on the user's current state, and generates a structured governance readiness report with a prioritized remediation roadmap.
The tool is designed for a Privacy Counsel, Legal Operations Manager, Chief Privacy Officer, or DPO at a company that uses AI internally and needs to understand their EU AI Act exposure — but does not have the budget for a Big 4 consulting engagement or an enterprise GRC platform.
Input format: A structured form (use case name, industry, deployment context, current safeguards in place) plus a free-text description field where the user describes the AI use case in their own words.
Output format: A structured PDF or on-screen report containing: risk tier classification with rationale, applicable EU AI Act articles and obligations, identified compliance gaps, and a prioritized remediation roadmap. Every output includes the mandatory disclaimer defined below.

Architectural Decisions (Do Not Override Without Explicit Review)
These decisions were made deliberately. If a build direction would require changing any of these, stop and flag it before implementing.
Input capped at 1,300 tokens total (500 form + 800 free text). Rationale: Cost control and predictable per-assessment pricing. Do not increase without recalculating cost model and pricing.


Haiku for validation/formatting, Sonnet for reasoning. Rationale: Significant cost reduction per assessment. Haiku at current pricing is ~8x cheaper than Sonnet per token. Do not use Sonnet for steps that do not require regulatory reasoning.


Streamlit is UI only — no business logic. Rationale: Front-end portability. Streamlit can be replaced with a proper web front-end later without rebuilding the core.


Supabase standard PostgreSQL only (no proprietary extensions). Rationale: Data portability. If Supabase pricing becomes problematic at scale, migration to any Postgres host is trivial.


EU AI Act as primary framework for POC. Rationale: Concrete classification tiers, active enforcement timeline, and genuine mid-market urgency. Framework-agnostic tools are harder to build well and harder to sell clearly. US frameworks to be layered in during Build 2.


No document uploads in Build 1. Rationale: Document parsing (PDFs, Word docs) introduces token unpredictability and technical complexity. Form + free text gives controlled input. Document ingestion is a Build 3 feature.


Assessment data deleted within 30 days unless user saves it. Rationale: Minimizes data breach exposure and simplifies privacy compliance. Do not extend this window without reviewing privacy policy implications.



EU AI Act Quick Reference (For Prompt Architecture)
Use this to inform how you instruct Claude to classify use cases. Do not treat this as legal advice — verify against EUR-Lex before building classification logic.
UNACCEPTABLE RISK (Prohibited):
Real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces (with narrow law enforcement exceptions)
Social scoring by public authorities
Exploitation of vulnerabilities of specific groups
Subliminal manipulation causing harm
HIGH RISK (Strict obligations — conformity assessment, registration, human oversight, technical documentation):
AI in safety components of products (machinery, medical devices, vehicles, infrastructure)
Biometric categorization and emotion recognition
Critical infrastructure management
Education and vocational training (access, evaluation)
Employment (recruitment, performance management, termination)
Essential services (credit scoring, insurance risk, emergency dispatch)
Law enforcement, migration, asylum, border control
Administration of justice and democratic processes
LIMITED RISK (Transparency obligations):
Chatbots and conversational AI (must disclose AI nature)
Deepfakes and synthetic content (must label)
Emotion recognition systems (must inform individuals)
MINIMAL RISK (No specific obligations under the Act):
AI-enabled video games
Spam filters
AI-powered inventory management
Most B2B AI productivity tools
GPAI (General Purpose AI Models — separate obligations under Title VIII):
Large generative AI models with systemic risk designation have additional obligations (incident reporting, adversarial testing)
Relevant if your customer's company is a GPAI provider, not just a downstream deployer
Enforcement timeline reference (verify currency):
February 2025: Prohibited practices provisions applied
August 2025: GPAI model obligations applied
August 2026: High-risk AI system obligations fully applied
August 2027: High-risk AI in existing products
